# Research Trend Report: Agentic AI in financial services

## Executive Summary
Recent literature suggests that agentic AI in financial services is moving from generic autonomous-agent framing toward domain-specific workflows such as research copilots, compliance support, risk triage, and structured decision assistance. The most promising systems combine retrieval, tool use, and evaluation safeguards rather than relying on unconstrained autonomy.

## Top Emerging Themes

### Tool-Using Financial Agents
- Description: Research is converging on agents that call tools, retrieve documents, and execute auditable multi-step workflows.
- Representative papers: arxiv:2501.00001, semanticscholar:abc123, openalex:https://openalex.org/W123
- Why this is emerging: Financial environments require explainability, traceability, and integration with existing systems.

### Evaluation and Safety
- Description: Many recent papers emphasize benchmarking agent reliability, hallucination control, and human review loops.
- Representative papers: crossref:10.5555/example-doi, semanticscholar:def456
- Why this is emerging: Production deployment in regulated settings raises a higher bar for validation.

## Methods and Techniques Being Used
- Retrieval-augmented generation
- Tool-use orchestration
- Human-in-the-loop validation
- Benchmark-driven evaluation

## Research Gaps
- Limited public benchmarks for regulated financial-agent tasks.
- Sparse long-horizon evaluation for multi-step decision workflows.
- Need for stronger evidence on human oversight patterns in production.

## Business / Product Implications
- Near-term value is strongest in analyst-assistive workflows rather than fully autonomous financial decisioning.
- Teams should invest in grounded retrieval, audit logs, and review checkpoints before scaling agentic systems.
